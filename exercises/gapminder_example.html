1 The data

In this report, we explore data from Gapminder. More specifically, we use an excerpt from these data that are included in the gapminder package for R. The data contains information about life expectancy, population, and GDP per capita for 142 countries from 5 different continents, spanning the time from 1952 to 2007.

2 Analyses

For our exploration, we will focus on data from the year 2007.

2.1 Life expectancy

In 2007, life expectancy ranged from 23.6 to 82.6, with a global average of 59.47.

gap_2007 %>% 
  ggplot(aes(x = lifeExp)) +
  geom_density(fill="#69b3a2",
               color="#e9ecef") +
  scale_y_continuous(expand=expansion(mult=c(0,0.1))) +
  scale_x_continuous(expand=expansion(mult=c(0,0))) +
  theme_minimal()

2.2 GDP per capita

The minimum GDP per capita in 2007 was 241.17, the maximum was 113523.13, and the worldwide mean was 7215.33.

As we can see, GDP per capita is heavily right-skewed.

2.3 Relationship between life expectancy and GDP per capita

We employ a bubble plot as made popular by the TED talk by Hans Rosling to visualize the relationship between GDP per capita and life expectancy in 2007. The plot also includes visual information about the continent on which a country is located as well as its population size.

gap_2007 %>% 
ggplot(aes(x = gdpPercap, y = lifeExp)) +
  geom_point(aes(size = pop,
                 color = continent),
             alpha = .5) +
  scale_x_log10() +
  labs(caption = "Due to the heavily skewed distribution of GDP per capita, the X-axis uses a log-scale")
